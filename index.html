At 01:02 UTC, a targeted spear-phishing email was delivered to a finance department user, containing a macro-enabled Word document (Invoice_Q1_2024.docm). Upon opening, an obfuscated VBA macro executed a PowerShell command to download a Cobalt Strike beacon from the attacker's command-and-control infrastructure located at 185.220.101.47:443.

Within 15 minutes, the implant performed automated network reconnaissance, sweeping the entire internal subnet. At 01:31 UTC, the malware established persistence by writing a registry Run key, ensuring survivability across reboots.

The attack escalated significantly at 01:44 UTC when the threat actor deployed Mimikatz directly in memory, successfully harvesting 14 NTLM password hashes — including two Domain Administrator accounts — from the LSASS process on the compromised workstation.

Armed with privileged credentials, the attacker executed a Pass-the-Hash attack at 02:09 UTC, authenticating laterally to Domain Controller DC-01 (10.0.1.12) without knowing the plaintext password. On DC-01, a rogue SYSTEM-level account was provisioned, granting permanent, hidden access to the domain.

The incident concluded with 2.3 GB of sensitive data exfiltrated via encrypted HTTPS tunneling to the external C2 server, bypassing perimeter DLP controls by blending with normal web traffic on port 443. The total dwell time from initial compromise to exfiltration was 72 minutes.
